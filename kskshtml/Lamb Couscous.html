Delicious Lamb Couscous Recipe

A hearty Mediterranean comfort meal, this lamb couscous combines tender, spiced lamb with fluffy couscous and vibrant vegetables. Perfect for family gatherings or a cozy dinner.

Ingredients

For the Lamb Stew

  • 1 lb boneless lamb shoulder or leg, cubed
  • 1 medium red onion, diced
  • 2 bell peppers, chopped
  • 2 tbsp honey
  • 2 tbsp soy sauce
  • 1 tsp dried mint
  • 3 green onions, sliced
  • 3 tbsp olive oil
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp paprika
  • 1 tsp coriander
  • 1 tsp cinnamon
  • 1/2 tsp ginger
  • 1/2 tsp Ras el Hanout (optional)
  • 1 cup vegetable broth
  • 1 cup diced tomatoes

For the Couscous

  • 1 cup pearl couscous
  • 1 1/2 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tbsp butter or olive oil
  • 1/2 tsp salt

For Garnishing

  • Fresh cilantro or mint, chopped
  • Lemon zest or wedges
  • Crumbled feta cheese (optional)
  • Toasted almonds or pine nuts

Instructions

1. Marinate and Sear the Lamb

  1. In a bowl, mix lamb cubes with cumin, paprika, coriander, cinnamon, ginger, and Ras el Hanout. Let marinate for 30 minutes to 2 hours.
  2. Heat 1 tbsp olive oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Sear lamb cubes until browned on all sides (about 3-4 minutes per side). Remove and set aside.

2. Cook the Couscous

  1. Bring 1 1/2 cups vegetable broth to a boil.
  2. Stir in pearl couscous, butter, and salt. Cover, reduce heat, and simmer for 10-15 minutes until the liquid is absorbed.
  3. Fluff with a fork and set aside.

3. Prepare the Lamb Stew

  1. In a large Dutch oven or pot, heat 2 tbsp olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add red onion, bell peppers, and garlic. Sauté for 4-5 minutes until soft.
  3. Stir in honey, soy sauce, diced tomatoes, and vegetable broth. Bring to a simmer.
  4. Return the seared lamb to the pot, cover, and bake at 450°F (230°C) for 20 minutes, or simmer on the stovetop for 30 minutes, until the lamb is tender.

4. Combine and Serve

  1. Gently fold cooked couscous into the lamb stew. Stir in green onions, lemon zest, and fresh herbs.
  2. Serve warm, garnished with toasted almonds, crumbled feta, and more fresh herbs.

Serving Suggestions

  • Serve with Moroccan salads, roasted vegetables, or yogurt-based sauces (tzatziki, labneh).
  • Enjoy with flatbread or pita to soak up the flavors.

Storage Tips

  • Store leftovers in an airtight container in the fridge for up to 3 days.
  • Reheat gently on the stove with a splash of broth to keep it moist.
