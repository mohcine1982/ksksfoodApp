Couscous Soup Recipe

This Mediterranean couscous soup is a quick, nourishing, and flavor-packed meal. The combination of fresh vegetables, warming spices, and fluffy couscous makes it a perfect choice for busy weeknights.

Ingredients

Base Ingredients

  • 1 cup cooked couscous
  • 4 cups vegetable or chicken broth
  • 1 (15 oz) can diced tomatoes
  • 1 cup zucchini, diced
  • ½ cup onion, diced
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced

Seasonings & Herbs

  • 1 tsp dried oregano
  • ½ tsp ground cumin (optional)
  • ½ tsp smoked paprika (optional)
  • Salt & black pepper to taste
  • Fresh parsley or cilantro for garnish

Optional Additions

  • 1 can (15 oz) chickpeas, drained & rinsed (for extra protein)
  • 1 cup baby spinach or kale (for added greens)
  • ½ tsp red pepper flakes (for a little heat)

Instructions

1. Sauté the Aromatics

  1. In a large saucepan, heat 1 tbsp olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add onions and garlic. Sauté for 2-3 minutes until fragrant.

2. Cook the Vegetables

  1. Stir in zucchini, diced tomatoes, and seasonings (oregano, cumin, paprika, salt, and pepper).
  2. Cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally, until the vegetables soften.

3. Add the Broth & Simmer

  1. Pour in vegetable or chicken broth and bring to a gentle boil.
  2. Reduce heat and let it simmer for 5-7 minutes.

4. Add Couscous & Final Touches

  1. Stir in the cooked couscous and mix well.
  2. Add any extra ingredients like chickpeas, spinach, or red pepper flakes at this stage.
  3. Simmer for 2 more minutes to blend the flavors.

5. Serve & Garnish

  1. Ladle the soup into bowls and garnish with fresh parsley or cilantro.
  2. Enjoy hot with crusty bread or a side salad.

Customizations & Variations

Vegetarian & Vegan Option

  • Use vegetable broth instead of chicken broth.
  • Add lentils, chickpeas, or tofu for extra plant-based protein.

Protein Add-ins

  • Try grilled chicken, shrimp, or crumbled feta cheese for a protein boost.

Seasonal Vegetable Swaps

  • Spring: Add asparagus, peas, or leeks.
  • Summer: Use fresh tomatoes, bell peppers, or corn.
  • Fall/Winter: Try carrots, butternut squash, or sweet potatoes.
