Here's a complete recipe for Mediterranean-Style Couscous with Roasted Carrots and Wilted Spinach:

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup couscous (quick-cooking variety)
  • 2 cups vegetable broth (or water)
  • 3 large carrots, diced
  • 2 tbsp olive oil (for roasting and sautéing)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 cups fresh spinach
  • 2 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1/4 cup fresh parsley (or basil), chopped
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese (optional)
  • 1/2 cup halved cherry tomatoes (optional)
  • 1 tbsp za'atar seasoning (optional, for extra flavor)

Instructions

  1. Roast the Carrots:

    • Preheat your oven to 400°F (200°C).
    • In a bowl, toss the diced carrots with 1 tablespoon of olive oil, salt, and pepper.
    • Spread the carrots evenly on a baking sheet.
    • Roast for 20-25 minutes, or until the carrots are tender and caramelized, stirring halfway through.

  2. Cook the Couscous:

    • While the carrots roast, bring the vegetable broth (or water) to a boil in a saucepan.
    • Once boiling, remove the pan from heat and add the couscous. Stir to combine.
    • Cover the pan and let the couscous steam for 5-7 minutes.
    • Fluff the couscous with a fork to separate the grains, then set aside.

  3. Wilt the Spinach:

    • In a large skillet, heat 1 tablespoon of olive oil over medium heat.
    • Add the minced garlic and sauté for about 1 minute, until fragrant.
    • Add the spinach in batches, stirring, until it wilts down (about 2-3 minutes).
    • Season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. Assemble the Dish:

    • In a large bowl, combine the roasted carrots, wilted spinach, and cooked couscous.
    • Gently toss to mix, then drizzle with lemon juice and add chopped parsley or basil.
    • If desired, top with halved cherry tomatoes, crumbled feta cheese, and a sprinkle of za'atar for extra flavor.

  5. Serve:

    • Serve warm or at room temperature. This dish makes a perfect light main course or a side dish.
