Authentic Tunisian Couscous Recipe

Tunisian couscous is a rich, flavorful dish that showcases the unique tastes of North Africa. It blends aromatic spices, tender meats, and fresh vegetables with semolina couscous to create a delightful meal that is both satisfying and nourishing.

Ingredients

  • Couscous: 2 cups of semolina couscous (preferably Tunisian-style for the best texture)
  • Olive oil: 2 tablespoons
  • Meat: 1 lb (450g) of lamb, chicken, or beef (cut into pieces)
  • Vegetables:
    • 2 carrots (peeled and sliced)
    • 2 zucchinis (sliced)
    • 1 turnip (peeled and chopped)
    • 1 large onion (chopped)
    • 2 tomatoes (chopped)
  • Spices:
    • 1 teaspoon cumin
    • 1 teaspoon coriander
    • 1 teaspoon caraway seeds
    • 1 teaspoon ground chili pepper (adjust to your spice preference)
    • 1-2 tablespoons harissa paste (optional, for extra heat)
  • Broth: 4 cups (vegetable or chicken broth)
  • Salt: to taste
  • Pepper: to taste
  • Traditional Garnishes:
    • 1/2 cup toasted almonds
    • 1/2 cup fried onions
    • 1/4 cup raisins
    • 2 tablespoons spiced olive oil (optional)
  • Harissa paste: for serving (optional, for those who like extra spice)

Preparation Method

1. Prepare the Couscous

  1. Rinse the couscous: Start by rinsing the couscous under cold water to remove any excess starch. This helps it cook up light and fluffy.
  2. Steam the couscous: Transfer the rinsed couscous to a steamer or colander placed over boiling water. Cover with a lid and steam for 15-20 minutes. Fluff the couscous with a fork every 5 minutes to ensure it’s evenly cooked.

2. Prepare the Meat and Vegetable Base

  1. Brown the meat: In a large pot, heat the olive oil over medium heat. Add the meat (lamb, chicken, or beef) and cook until browned on all sides. Remove and set aside.
  2. Cook the vegetables: In the same pot, add the onion and sauté for 2-3 minutes until softened. Then add the carrots, zucchini, and turnip. Cook for an additional 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the spices: Stir in the cumin, coriander, caraway, chili pepper, and harissa paste. Mix well to coat the vegetables and release the spices’ fragrance.
  4. Add the tomatoes: Stir in the chopped tomatoes and let them cook down for 5 minutes.
  5. Simmer the broth: Add the broth (vegetable or chicken) and bring to a simmer. Let the stew cook for 30-45 minutes until the meat is tender and the vegetables are cooked through.

3. Assemble the Couscous

  1. Combine couscous and stew: Once the couscous is steamed, transfer it to a large, shallow M'semmen (or a large bowl). Pour the vegetable and meat stew over the couscous.
  2. Garnish: Top with toasted almonds, fried onions, raisins, and a drizzle of spiced olive oil for extra flavor.

4. Serving

Serve the couscous and stew in the traditional way, allowing guests to help themselves. It’s customary to eat with hands from the communal bowl in Tunisia. Offer harissa paste on the side for anyone who likes extra heat.
