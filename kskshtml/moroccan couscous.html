Authentic Moroccan Couscous Recipe

Ingredients

For the Couscous:

  • 2 cups couscous (medium grain)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 ½ cups water

For the Broth & Meat:

  • 2 lbs lamb or beef (cut into chunks)
  • 2 tablespoons olive oil
  • 1 large onion (chopped)
  • 3 tomatoes (grated or finely chopped)
  • 6 cups water
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon black pepper
  • 1 teaspoon turmeric
  • 1 teaspoon ground ginger
  • ½ teaspoon saffron threads (optional)
  • 1 cinnamon stick (optional)
  • 1 small bunch of parsley and cilantro (tied together)

For the Vegetables:

  • 2 carrots (peeled and cut into halves)
  • 2 zucchini (cut into large pieces)
  • 1 turnip (peeled and quartered)
  • 1 cup cabbage (roughly chopped)
  • 1 cup pumpkin or butternut squash (cubed)
  • 1 can chickpeas (drained)

For Serving:

  • ½ cup raisins (optional, soaked in warm water)
  • ½ cup almonds (toasted)
  • Harissa sauce (optional, for spice)

Preparation Method

Step 1: Preparing the Broth & Meat

  1. In a large pot or couscoussier, heat 2 tablespoons of olive oil over medium heat.
  2. Add the chopped onions and cook until soft.
  3. Add the meat and brown it on all sides.
  4. Stir in grated tomatoes, salt, black pepper, turmeric, ginger, saffron, and cinnamon.
  5. Pour in 6 cups of water and add the parsley and cilantro bunch.
  6. Cover and let it simmer for 45 minutes (or until the meat is tender).

Step 2: Cooking the Vegetables

  1. Add the carrots, turnips, and cabbage to the pot and continue cooking for 15 minutes.
  2. Then, add the zucchini, pumpkin, and chickpeas, and cook for another 15 minutes.

Step 3: Preparing the Couscous

  1. In a large bowl, mix couscous grains with olive oil and salt.
  2. Gradually add 1 cup of water, rubbing the grains between your hands to separate them.
  3. Place the couscous in the steaming basket of the couscoussier (or a steamer over the broth pot).
  4. Cover and steam for 15 minutes.
  5. Remove, transfer to a large bowl, and fluff with a fork. Sprinkle with another ½ cup of water, mix well, and steam again for 15 minutes.
  6. Repeat the process one more time for a total of three steamings, ensuring a light and fluffy texture.

Step 4: Assembling & Serving

  1. Spread the cooked couscous onto a large serving platter.
  2. Arrange the meat and vegetables on top.
  3. Pour some of the flavorful broth over the dish to moisten it.
  4. Garnish with raisins and toasted almonds, and serve with harissa sauce on the side.
