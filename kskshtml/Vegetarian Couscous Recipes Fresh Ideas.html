Chicken Couscous with Sweet Apricot Preserves

This flavorful Moroccan chicken couscous is a feast for the senses, combining tender marinated chicken, fluffy couscous, and the sweet and tangy touch of apricot preserves. It's perfect for a hearty family meal or an exotic dinner party.

Ingredients 

  • Boneless, skinless chicken thighs: 1.5 lbs (680g)
  • Couscous: 2 cups
  • Onion: 1 large (chopped)
  • Garlic: 3 cloves (minced)
  • Ground cumin: 1 teaspoon
  • Ground cinnamon: 1 teaspoon
  • Paprika: 1 teaspoon
  • Salt: to taste
  • Black pepper: to taste
  • Apricot preserves: 1/4 cup
  • Olive oil: 2 tablespoons (for cooking)

Kitchen Equipment Needed

  • Large skillet or Dutch oven
  • Medium saucepan
  • Whisk or fork (for fluffing couscous)
  • Measuring cups and spoons
  • Cutting board and knife

Preparation Method

1. Marinate the Chicken

  1. Marinate the chicken: In a bowl, combine the cumin, cinnamon, paprika, salt, and pepper. Add a tablespoon of olive oil and mix well. Coat the chicken thighs with this spice mixture. Add apricot preserves to the marinade and mix until evenly coated.
  2. Let the chicken marinate for at least 1 hour, or even better, refrigerate and marinate overnight for deeper flavor.

2. Prepare the Couscous

  1. Cook the couscous: Bring 2 cups of water to a boil in a medium saucepan. Add a pinch of salt. Stir in the couscous, remove from heat, and cover. Let it sit for 5 minutes to steam.
  2. Fluff the couscous: After the couscous has absorbed the water, use a fork to fluff the grains, separating them gently. This will ensure the couscous is light and fluffy.

3. Cook the Chicken

  1. Heat olive oil in a large skillet or Dutch oven over medium heat. Once hot, add the marinated chicken and sear it for 5-7 minutes per side, until golden brown and fully cooked. The internal temperature should reach 165°F (74°C). Remove the chicken from the skillet and set aside.
  2. Sauté the onion and garlic in the same skillet until softened, about 3-4 minutes.
  3. Add apricot preserves: Stir in the apricot preserves and let it cook for an additional 2 minutes until it becomes a thick, sweet glaze.

4. Assemble the Dish

  1. Serve the couscous: Place the fluffy couscous on a large round, shallow bowl or a traditional tagine.
  2. Top with chicken: Slice the cooked chicken into strips and arrange it on top of the couscous.
  3. Add extra apricot glaze from the pan over the chicken for added sweetness and flavor.

5. Garnish and Presentation

  1. Garnish with toasted almonds, golden raisins, and a drizzle of spiced olive oil for a Moroccan touch.
  2. Serve the couscous alongside roasted vegetables, a mint-cucumber salad, or a spiced chickpea stew to complete the meal.

Greek-Style Couscous with Feta and Olives

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup cooked couscous
  • 1/2 cup crumbled feta cheese
  • 1/2 cup cherry tomatoes, halved
  • 1/4 cup Kalamata olives, pitted and halved
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tsp chopped fresh oregano
  • Salt and pepper to taste

Preparation Method:

  1. Cook the Couscous: Begin by cooking 1 cup of couscous according to package instructions. Typically, this involves bringing 1 cup of water or vegetable broth to a boil, then stirring in the couscous, covering it, and letting it steam for 5-10 minutes until fluffy.

  2. Prepare the Vegetables and Cheese: While the couscous is cooking, halve the cherry tomatoes, pit and halve the Kalamata olives, and crumble the feta cheese.

  3. Mix the Ingredients: In a large bowl, combine the cooked couscous, feta cheese, cherry tomatoes, and olives.

  4. Make the Dressing: In a small bowl, whisk together the lemon juice, olive oil, chopped oregano, salt, and pepper.

  5. Toss and Serve: Pour the dressing over the couscous mixture and toss everything together until well combined.

  6. Taste and Adjust: Taste your salad and adjust seasoning if needed, adding more salt, pepper, or lemon juice to suit your taste.
